California renewal fees

What makes up your renewal total?

California registration renewal fees are determined by several factors including vehicle value, weight, county, and year. Here's what typically appears on your renewal:

Registration fee

A base fee charged by the state for processing the annual renewal of your vehicle's registration. The amount varies by vehicle type and weight class.

Vehicle license fee (VLF)

Calculated at 0.65% of the estimated value of your vehicle. This fee decreases each year as the vehicle depreciates. It may be deductible on your federal taxes.

County / district fees

Local fees collected on behalf of your county or district. These vary by location and may fund local transportation or environmental programs.

Smog abatement fee

Vehicles less than 6 years old that are exempt from a smog inspection pay a $20 abatement fee instead. Other exemptions include electric vehicles, hybrids, and pre-1975 vehicles.

Smog inspection exemptions

The following vehicles do not require a biennial smog check:

  • Electric and hydrogen fuel-cell vehicles
  • Hybrid vehicles (gasoline/electric)
  • Motorcycles and trailers
  • Vehicles manufactured before 1976
  • Diesel vehicles manufactured before 1998
  • Vehicles less than 6 model years old (pay $20 abatement fee instead)
  • Natural gas vehicles over 14,000 lbs GVWR
Renewal reminders & timing

When should you renew?

California law requires you to renew your vehicle registration on or before the expiration date shown on your current sticker.

Renewal reminders

The CA DMV mails renewal notices to your registered address roughly 60 days before your expiration date. If you don't receive one, you're still responsible for renewing on time. We'll email you a confirmation once your renewal is processed.

Late renewal & grace period

California does not offer a grace period — your registration expires on the date printed on your sticker. Driving with expired tags can result in a citation. Late penalties apply if you renew after the expiration date.

Planned non-operation (PNO)

If your vehicle will not be operated on public roads for a full year, you can file a Planned Non-Operation (PNO) to avoid paying full registration fees. The vehicle must be parked off public roads for the entire period.

Renewal period

Most California registrations are renewed annually (12 months). Renewal is tied to your vehicle's initial registration month. Completing your renewal early does not shorten your next renewal cycle.
